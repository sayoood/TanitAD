## 1. Executive verdict

TanitAD is, on the evidence in this repository, **a genuinely well-run empirical program with a
world-class measurement culture and a serious, unresolved credibility gap between what it has measured
and what it claims.**

Three things are true simultaneously, and the program's health depends on holding all three at once.

**First — the science is real and unusually honest.** The flagship v1 result (ADE@2s **0.4522 ± 0.0312**
heldout, `MODEL_REGISTRY.md §1.2`) is not a leaderboard artefact: it clears the *honest* floors the
program itself constructed to make it hard (best-of-3 kinematic 0.5005, CTRV oracle 0.523, learned
no-vision ego-status ceiling 0.5735), and it is backed by a **causal** vision-attribution panel
(vision effect **+1.325 m, CI [+1.04, +1.64]**, `TANITAD_PAPER.md §7.3`) rather than an aggregate.
Building your own ego-status shortcut ceiling and then beating it is what separates this program from
most open-loop AD papers. The frozen-encoder negative (H4) is likewise clean and publishable: REF-A
improves *monotonically* to 30 k (5 k 3.755 → 30 k 2.920, `MODEL_REGISTRY.md §2.3`) and still plateaus,
which distinguishes a capability ceiling from overfitting — a distinction most teams never earn because
they never keep milestone checkpoints (D-032).

**Second — the program's single most important measured result is that its own architecture's decision
layer does not work, and the program has correctly pivoted on it.** The flagship's supervised tactical
head scores **3.38 m** — worse than constant velocity — while the *same model's* operative rollout
scores 0.452 m (`MODEL_REGISTRY.md §6`, last row). A **training-free** CEM planner over the frozen world
model beats that head by +2.257 ± 0.329 m open-loop and cuts closed-loop drift 38 % (P2, `§5`). That is
a first-rate piece of science: zero GPU-days spent, a decisive architectural verdict obtained, and the
v3 pivot (D-033) is properly grounded in it. Few industrial teams would have run the cheap experiment
first.

**Third — and this is the part a board must not be allowed to skim: the headline claims are
in-distribution claims, and the program's own OOD panel says the model does not generalize.** On
comma2k19 the flagship scores 0.849 against a 0.372 floor and wins **17.5 %** of windows; on Cosmos
0.583 vs 0.358, 29.4 % (`TANITAD_PAPER.md §7.4`). Open-loop 0.452 becomes closed-loop **1.685**
(`MODEL_REGISTRY.md §1.2`). The paper reports both honestly — genuinely to its credit — but the
*program's* framing ("first sub-floor arm", "the world model is real") has drifted ahead of the
evidence, and the abstract still leads with the in-distribution number. **On today's evidence TanitAD
has demonstrated a strong in-distribution open-loop predictor on a single 2,376-episode corpus, and has
*not* demonstrated a driving stack.** The distance between those two statements is the entire remaining
program.

**The dominant institutional risk is not scientific — it is operational.** As of the 2026-07-20 audit,
the entire TanitEval harness, the P2 planner that justifies the v3 pivot, and REF-B v2's architecture
existed on single pod disks with no copy in git (`AGENT_OPERATING_STANDARD.md`, evidence table). They
were rescued the same day (`a91bef8`), which is the right response — but the *system* that produced the
failure (concurrent single-writer git, prose-vs-registry drift, two commit-sweep misattributions in one
day per `CLAUDE.md` "Git hygiene", a boot hook that nearly double-launched trainers onto one GPU per
`60265d3`) is only partially fixed. A research org of this quality should not be one pod-loss from
losing its evidence base.

**Methodologically, one specific practice is not defensible and is currently steering multi-GPU-day
decisions: the learning-curve power-law exponent gate.** v2 was killed on it (D-031); v3enc's
pre-registered falsifier is stated in it (D-A7); and v3enc's own pre-5k exponent (−0.529, **R² = 0.373**,
n = 60) now sits at the level of the arm that was killed. Fitting a power law to a *training* metric over
a *short, early* window, with no confidence interval on the exponent and no guarantee that the reference
exponents were fit over matched step ranges, is not a decision-grade instrument. §6 P1 specifies the
replacement. The *decision* to kill v2 was probably right for other reasons; the *instrument* used to
justify it should not be reused as-is.

**Verdict in one line:** the measurement culture is top-decile, the core world-model result is real and
causally attributed, the architecture pivot is correctly evidenced — and the program is over-indexed on
in-distribution open-loop numbers, running a decision gate that cannot bear the weight placed on it,
with an operational substrate that has already lost work twice and would lose more tomorrow.

## 3. Strengths that survive scrutiny

These are the things I actively tried to break and could not.

**S1 — The floor construction is better than the field's. 🟢**
Most open-loop AD work compares to constant velocity and stops. This program built **four** floors of
increasing hostility — CV 0.8248, CTRV oracle 0.523, best-of-3 kinematic 0.5005, and a *learned* ridge
ego-status ceiling 0.5735 (`MODEL_REGISTRY.md §0.3`) — and then held itself to the hardest. It
independently replicated the ego-status shortcut result (AD-MLP/BEV-Planner, arXiv 2312.03031) on its own
corpus, measuring 73.9 % straight driving — *coincidentally identical to nuScenes*
(`HYPOTHESIS_LEDGER.md`, 2026-07-17 Benchmarks entry). Two independently implemented instrument lines
converged on CTRV 0.544 / 0.545 (`TANITAD_PAPER.md §7.2`). **This is replication discipline, and it is
the single strongest signal that the 0.452 m number means something.**

**S2 — The vision-attribution panel is a causal claim, properly constructed. 🟢**
`TANITAD_PAPER.md §7.3`: stratify by CTRV divergence (isolating the windows where anticipation, not
extrapolation, is required), then mean-replace the scene. The advantage +0.796 m **inverts to −0.529 m**;
vision effect **+1.325 m, CI [+1.04, +1.64]**, CI-separated. Supported by three independent
corroborations — monotone in divergence (Q1 −0.372 → Q4 +0.796), curvature decodable from the pooled
latent at R² 0.254 vs 0.031 ego-only, and road-ahead occlusion perturbing 1.60× more than an equal-area
periphery patch. That is four different instruments agreeing. **I cannot construct an alternative
explanation that survives all four.** This is the program's best result and it is stronger than the
aggregate ADE it is usually quoted alongside.

**S3 — H4 was closed with a *ceiling* proof, not a *plateau assertion*. 🟢**
The distinction matters and almost nobody earns it. Because D-032 archived milestones at 5k/15k/20k/30k,
REF-A's curve is readable: **3.755 → 3.694 → 3.016 → 2.920, best is last** (`MODEL_REGISTRY.md §2.3`).
Held-out error never rises, so overfitting is excluded and the plateau is a capability ceiling. Every
remedy was tried first (speed, yaw, dyn-input `[v0,yr0]`, ego-dropout, temporal adapter, 4 brains, I-JEPA
features). And when the first read ("frozen DINO is unfit") was wrong, the program *corrected itself* to
the sharper claim — a per-frame semantic encoder neither does nor should encode yaw, so frozen-DINO was
**mis-wired, not unfit** (`HYPOTHESIS_LEDGER.md`, 2026-07-18 reframe). **Self-correction against your own
prior conclusion is the rarest thing on this list.**

**S4 — P2: the right experiment, run first, for zero GPU-days. 🟢**
Rather than train a v3 to test whether planning beats heads, the program ran a **training-free** CEM
planner over the *frozen* v1 world model, fit nothing, and engineered the cost from physical scales
explicitly to avoid circularity (`MODEL_REGISTRY.md §5`: "engineered from physical scales, not fit to GT
ADE — fitting would make G1 circular"). Both gates passed CI-separated (G1 0.893 vs head 3.150; G4 1.038
vs 1.685), **and** it was robustness-checked across a 3×3 weight sweep spanning a 4× band, moving ADE only
3.4 % and winning all 9 configs. The honest signature is reported too: 66 % of its residual is lateral,
correctly attributed to the cost having no lateral term by design (D-A10). **This is how architecture
decisions should be made.**

**S5 — The aleatoric/epistemic separation on the oracle gap. 🟢**
This is the most sophisticated piece of statistics in the repo. The program had a tempting story —
"oracle-in-fan 0.164 vs selected 0.471, so there is 0.307 m of headroom in the ranker" — and then
*killed its own story* with a train-fit ceiling argument: across **47 trained arms** the best recovery of
the gap **on the data it is actively fitting** is 8.4 %, capacity is not binding (smaller heads are
worse), and dev tracks train so it is not overfitting
(`2026-07-20-refc-v12-learned-rescorer.md §4.4`). The chance floors make it interpretable (chance over
256 = 13.96 m; chance inside top-8 = 1.034 m; frozen confidence already captures **67.7 %** of the
chance→oracle span). The retraction is explicit and signed in the registry: *"An earlier revision of this
section (mine) claimed 'oracle within top-8 = 87 % of the gap, so fund the learned ranker'. That framing
was wrong"* (`MODEL_REGISTRY.md §4.1`). **A program that retracts its own headline in its own registry is
a program whose other numbers I am willing to believe.** (I do have a residual methodological criticism —
see W8 — but the direction of the conclusion holds.)

**S6 — Negative and null results are ranked equal to positive ones. 🟢**
The record contains: cost re-ranking recovers **0.0 %** (`7227537`); a GT-perfect speed-matcher scores
*worse* than baseline (1.1236); goal conditioning is **inert**, −0.006 m inside CI, while imagination
carries 96 % of the gain (`fc2c484`); `refined_conf` selection is 2.9× worse; hard-argmin is the worst
target in all five feature configurations. These are published in commit titles, not buried. **The v1.5
attribution commit (`fc2c484`) reporting "goal conditioning is inert" is the program telling on its own
design.**

**S7 — Reconstruction discipline as an explicit acceptance test. 🟢**
`MODEL_REGISTRY.md`'s framing — *"a reader with this repo + the pods can rebuild that exact model … Where
that chain is broken, the row says so in a RECONSTRUCTION RISK block rather than pretending"* — with a
✅/⚠️/🟥 verification legend and a 10-row gap register (§7), is better practice than most industrial ML
orgs achieve. The number-hygiene note resolving 0.628 vs 0.615 vs 0.640 into *heldout / full-set / derived
arithmetic* (§1.2) is exactly the kind of forensic care that prevents a paper retraction.

**S8 — Fail-loud, gated-lever engineering. 🟢**
Levers default off and are byte-identical when off (`refb.py`: `--speed-input` gated so old checkpoints
resume; `refc_train.py --labels {v1,v21}` defaults to **v1 so XL stays byte-reproducible**, `08c48af`).
Tests pin *mechanisms* rather than outputs: `soft(τ→0) == hard` numerically, and a gradient test showing
the soft target's gradient is 0 at a 0.300-vs-0.301 m pair while hard CE still pushes ≈0.5
(`refc-v12` §3.3). The zero-init re-scorer reproducing the frozen baseline **to the digit** (0.47143933
vs 0.4714393) is an identity check most teams never write.

## 4. Weaknesses & mistakes — severity-ranked, cited, each with the fix

### 🔴 W1 — The "8-split episode-disjoint jackknife" is not a jackknife, and its CI is anti-conservative

**Evidence.** `taniteval/taniteval/bench.py:109-115` draws 8 *independent random* 20 % episode holdouts
(`split_by_episode(eid, val_frac, s) for s in range(seed, seed+n_splits)`), scores each, and at
`bench.py:82-90` reports

```python
"ci95": round(float(1.96 * np.nanstd(v) / max(1, len(v)) ** .5), 4)
```

Three distinct problems:

1. **It is not a jackknife.** A jackknife is a *systematic* delete-1 (or delete-d) resampling whose
   variance estimator carries an inflation factor. This is **Monte-Carlo cross-validation / repeated
   random subsampling**. The name appears in the registry (`§0.3`), the paper, `PROGRAM_OVERVIEW §5.1`
   and every research note. A reviewer who checks the code will find the label wrong, and will then
   distrust everything else.
2. **The √n divisor treats overlapping, correlated splits as independent.** The 8 splits are drawn
   from the *same* 40 episodes and share episodes pairwise; `std/√8` is the standard error of the mean
   of 8 *correlated* estimates and understates the true dispersion. Worse, the quantity it converges to
   is just the full-set mean, so the interval describes *split-selection noise*, not uncertainty about
   the model's performance on driving.
3. **The relevant sample size is 40 episodes, not 881 windows and not 8 splits.** Windows within an
   episode are strongly dependent (stride 8 over a 199-frame clip). Approximating: each split averages
   ~8 episodes, so the across-split σ ≈ σ_ep/√8, and the reported half-width ≈ 1.96·σ_ep/8, whereas a
   correct episode-cluster SE over 40 episodes is ≈ 1.96·σ_ep/√40. **The published CI is roughly 20 %
   narrower than a correct cluster interval** — systematically anti-conservative, in the direction that
   makes "CI-separated" claims easier to declare.

**Why this is 🔴 and not 🟡:** "CI-separated" is the program's decision predicate. It gates D-A5
(H4 closure), the vision-effect headline, the P2 gates, the H26 seam verdicts, and the v3enc acceptance
criteria.

**Mitigating fact, stated fairly.** The *most load-bearing* claims do not depend on this estimator. The
vision effect, the speed A/B (+2.21 m [2.04, 2.39]), the REF-A-vs-flagship comparison (+2.62 m
[2.447, 2.798]) and the refc-v12 verdict are **paired** tests, and refc-v12 explicitly uses an
**episode-clustered** paired CI (`2026-07-20-refc-v12-learned-rescorer.md §4.5`:
"paired CI95 (episode-clustered) [−0.00616, +0.02500]"). Paired, episode-clustered tests are the right
instrument and are far more robust to this defect. So the damage is bounded to *single-arm* interval
claims — but those are what the registry leaderboard publishes.

**The fix (cheap, ~1 day, no GPU).**
- Rename the statistic honestly: `repeated_holdout` or `mc_cv`, everywhere.
- Replace the single-arm CI with an **episode-cluster bootstrap**: resample the 40 episodes with
  replacement, B = 2000, recompute the full-set metric, report the 2.5/97.5 percentiles. This is ~15
  lines and reuses the machinery already present in `refc_rerank`.
- Keep the dual `heldout` / `full_set` reporting — that part is genuinely good practice and should be
  preserved.
- Merge and use the stranded D1 statistical-power audit (`35d1041`, branch
  `worktree-agent+bench-eval-20260711`) — it was built for exactly this and its verdict ("the step-21k
  regression sits inside the estimator's noise band") is the same lesson.

### 🔴 W2 — The learning-curve power-law exponent gate is not decision-grade, and it is about to consume a second run

This is the highest-value finding in this review, because a live decision hangs on it today.

**What the gate is.** `2026-07-19-flagshipv2-6k-diagnostic.md:140-151` fits a power law to the *training*
metric `g_op_fwd_ade_m` over steps 1.5k–7.5k, obtains exponent **−0.50** for v2 against **−0.84** for v1,
extrapolates to 30 k (v2 0.273 vs v1's actual 0.030), concludes "~9× worse for the same ~4 days of A40",
and recommends the restart. D-031 adopts it; D-A7 makes it the pre-registered falsifier for v3enc.

**Live status (coordinator-supplied telemetry, 2026-07-20 — not yet in a repo artefact, flagged
UNVERIFIED-IN-REPO):** v3enc crossed step 5,000 with a pre-5k exponent of **−0.48 to −0.60 (best fit
−0.529, R² = 0.373, n = 60)** — i.e. **statistically indistinguishable from the −0.50 that killed v2.**
Applied literally, the gate kills v3enc too, and the program enters an unbounded restart loop.

**Why the instrument does not bear the weight placed on it — five defects:**

1. **R² = 0.373 means the power law does not describe the data.** 63 % of the variance is unexplained.
   An exponent estimated from such a fit is a weakly-identified parameter, and quoting it to two decimal
   places is false precision. The brief notes fits in the 0.1–0.4 R² band have steered restarts; at
   R² = 0.13 the fit is essentially a slope through noise.
2. **No confidence interval on the exponent is ever reported.** Anywhere. A parameter with unreported
   uncertainty is steering multi-GPU-day decisions. With n = 60 noisy points and R² = 0.37, a bootstrap
   CI on the slope would very plausibly span −0.3 to −0.8 — i.e. **overlap v1's −0.84 and fail to
   separate from v2's −0.50 simultaneously**, making the comparison vacuous.
3. **Step-range confounding — the comparison may be apples-to-oranges.** The v2 note states its fit range
   ("Power-law fit (1.5k–7.5k)") but the v1 reference exponent **−0.84 is quoted with no stated range**
   (`:151`). Training curves are not scale-free in practice: the earliest phase is steepest and the tail
   flattens, so an exponent depends strongly on the window. v3enc's is fit **pre-5k**, a *different*
   window again. Until all three are refit over identical step ranges the ordering −0.84 / −0.50 / −0.53
   is uninterpretable — and note the direction of the concern: v3enc's window *includes* the steepest
   early phase and should therefore be biased **more** negative, yet it still lands at −0.53.
4. **It fits a train metric to predict a val outcome.** `g_op_fwd_ade_m` comes from `train_log.jsonl`.
   The decision being made is about held-out ADE@2s at 30 k. Training-loss slope conflates optimization
   speed with generalization, and the two demonstrably dissociate here — REF-A's train fwd-ADE is 0.65
   against a held-out 2.92 (**a 4.5× gap**, `MODEL_REGISTRY.md §2.3`).
5. **It extrapolates 4–6× beyond the fitted range** from a poor fit, and it is applied at multiple
   milestones without any multiple-testing correction — a garden of forking paths in which some arm will
   always look bad at some gate.

**The ruling.** ❌ **The exponent gate is not defensible as a kill criterion.** It is acceptable only as
a soft diagnostic that triggers a closer look.

**But — and this materially changes the verdict on D-031 — the v2 kill was over-determined and was
probably correct for a different reason in the same note.** `:130-138` reports the **same-step ratio**
v2/v1 on identical metric and identical steps, widening 1.51 → 4.33, and the observation that **v1
reached v2's 7.5k value at step ~250 (~30× faster)**. Those are *matched-step comparisons*: they need no
power-law assumption, no extrapolation, and no exponent. They are robust and they are damning on their
own. **The right evidence was in the document; the wrong statistic got the headline and became the
precedent.** Fix the headline, keep the decision.

**The rigorous replacement (pre-register before the next launch):**

| Element | Prescription |
|---|---|
| **Metric** | **Held-out** val ADE@2s at archived milestones (D-032 already archives 5k/15k/20k/30k — the infrastructure exists). Never a train-log slope. |
| **Statistic** | **Matched-step ratio** r(s) = M_new(s) / M_ref(s) at *identical* s, with an **episode-cluster bootstrap CI** on r(s) (per W1). |
| **Decision rule** | Pre-register a single gate step and a numeric threshold τ *before launch*: continue iff the **lower** bound of the CI on r(s) < τ. Fix τ = 1.5 (the value implicitly used at the v2 10k gate: "≤ 2.5 m ≈ 3× CV"). |
| **Budget normalization** | Compare at equal **GPU-hours**, not equal steps. v3enc runs at **10.2 s/step** (`9d3074a`); if the reference ran faster per step, equal-step comparison flatters the new arm and understates its true cost. |
| **If an exponent is kept at all** | (a) refit **all** arms over **identical** step windows; (b) report a **bootstrap CI on the slope**; (c) require **R² ≥ 0.8** before the exponent may be quoted at all — below that, declare the power law unsupported and fall back to the ratio; (d) never extrapolate more than **2×** beyond the fitted range. |
| **Multiplicity** | One pre-registered gate step, or an explicit group-sequential boundary. Not "look at every milestone and decide". |
| **Anti-regress guard** | Cap the program at **two** restarts per lever family; a third failure means the lever family is refuted, not that the schedule needs more tuning. This is the structural protection against the loop v3enc is about to enter. |

**Immediate recommendation for v3enc:** do **not** kill on the pre-5k exponent. Run to the **10 k
milestone**, evaluate on held-out ADE@2s, and apply the pre-registered numeric gate that already exists
in the v2 diagnostic (`:196-199`: ADE@2s ≤ 2.5 m; encoder speed-probe R² ≥ 0.55; high-speed long
overshoot ≤ 8 m). Those three are val-side, numeric, and pre-registered — they are the good gate. The
exponent should be demoted to a logged diagnostic.

### 🔴 W3 — The program's headline claims are in-distribution claims, and OOD says the model does not generalize

**Evidence.** `TANITAD_PAPER.md §7.4`: comma2k19 (real highway, n = 2176) model **0.849** vs floor
**0.372**, per-window win-rate **17.5 %**; Cosmos (synthetic, n = 92) **0.583** vs **0.358**, 29.4 %.
Error roughly doubles; the anticipation advantage — the §7.3 headline — **collapses 0.80 → 0.15 m**;
path feasibility on sharp-curvature windows falls **97.8 % → 62.8 %**. Closed-loop:
open-loop 0.452 → **1.685**, divergence > 5 m on **22.2 %** of windows, and per
`PROGRAM_OVERVIEW §7`, **59 % of steps exceed the 2.0 m/s² comfort bound**.

**The criticism is not that this is hidden — it is reported, prominently, and that is to the program's
great credit.** The criticism is about **weighting**. The abstract leads with 0.452 and the OOD result
arrives as a subordinate clause after ~30 lines of positive result. `PROGRAM_OVERVIEW §1` marks C1
("It drives") as *"🔶 Open-loop **cleared**"*. The registry calls flagship v1 **"THE DEPLOYED MODEL"** /
*"✅ DEPLOYED / operative arm"* (`§1.2`) — nothing is deployed on a vehicle, and an external reader will
not know that. A model that loses to a two-parameter kinematic extrapolation on **both** unseen corpora,
and whose feasible-path rate collapses to 62.8 % OOD, has not cleared a driving bar in any sense a
regulator or a reviewer would accept.

**The deeper structural point:** the program measures OOD *after* optimizing in-distribution. D-A8
already contains the right instinct — *"Acceptance gate for v3enc should be the OOD panel, not
in-distribution … Optimizing the passed gate teaches nothing"* — but it is a **proposal in a decision
log**, not an enforced gate. v3enc's live gates are still the in-distribution ones.

**The fix.**
- **Promote D-A8 from proposal to binding gate.** No arm is "accepted" without an OOD panel row. Target
  as already proposed: comma2k19 floor win-rate ≥ 35 % (from 17.5 %).
- **Rewrite the abstract's claim ladder** so the bounded claim leads: *in-distribution, causally
  vision-attributed prediction*; then the honest limit. The §8 discussion already does this correctly —
  hoist that framing forward.
- **Retire the words "deployed" and "operative arm"** for a checkpoint that has never driven anything.
  Use "reference arm v1" / "current best arm".
- **Report closed-loop and feasibility alongside every headline ADE**, not in a separate section. A
  single table with `[in-dist ADE | OOD win-rate | closed-loop ADE | feasibility]` per arm makes
  cherry-picking structurally impossible.

### 🔴 W4 — The route label is circular *and* horizon-degenerate; a data defect was diagnosed as an architecture defect and treated with architecture levers

This is the most consequential technical mistake I found, and it is partially known but its *cost* has
not been accounted.

**Defect (a) — circularity.** `stack/scripts/refb_labels.py:172-175`:

```python
def route_target(nav_cmd: int) -> int:
    return _NAV_TO_ROUTE[nav_cmd]
```

The strategic head is **fed** `nav_cmd` and **trained to predict** a deterministic function of that same
`nav_cmd`. The target contains zero information not already in the input. The consequence is visible in
the logs and was not treated as an alarm: REF-B v2 final metrics record **`route_acc 1.00`**
(`MODEL_REGISTRY.md §3.5`). **A head scoring exactly 1.00 should always trigger an investigation.**
The flagship's counterpart metric, `route_skill_vs_chance = 0.0`, was correctly read as "pure command
echo" — but attributed to the *head* rather than to the *target*.

**Defect (b) — horizon degeneracy.** `refb_labels.py:72-73` sets `NAV_HORIZON_STEPS = 250` (25 s) and
`NAV_MIN_STEPS = 150` (15 s). PhysicalAI episodes are **199 frames = 19.9 s**
(`stack/tanitad/lake/vtarget.py:14-16` states this explicitly). Therefore:
- the 25 s horizon is **never** reached for any window;
- a window whose last input frame is at index L has 198 − L future frames, so `valid=True` requires
  **L ≤ 48** — roughly the first quarter of every episode;
- every other window returns `(NAV_FOLLOW, False)` (`:163`) and is **excluded from the route CE**.

**This is confirmed by the program's own measurement:** commit `a0645a7` records route label
**"coverage 26 % → 82 %"** for the v2.1 fix. 26 % matches the ~25 % predicted by the L ≤ 48 arithmetic
almost exactly.

**The cost.** D-A1 (07-11) had already identified "the strategic head had been training on a constant
`follow`" and fixed it with per-window derivation — but the horizon mismatch meant **~74 % of windows
still returned `follow`**, so the fix was only a quarter effective and nobody re-checked. The residual
"command-echo strategic head" then became one of the three named justifications for the **v2 lever pack**
(D-A6), motivating `v2_route_from_vision` (weight 0.3, an extra full strategic forward pass per step,
`flagship_losses.py:337-342`) and `v2_nav_dropout = 0.5`. v2 was then killed because **ten levers at once
made optimization too hard** (D-031). **A fraction of the ~4 GPU-days spent on v2, and the restart into
v3enc, is attributable to architecture levers compensating for a label bug.**

**How did it ship?** Because label *derivation* was unit-tested for correctness-of-logic but never
validated for *coverage and degeneracy against the actual corpus*. There is no test asserting
"fraction of windows with `valid=True` ≥ X on the parity corpus", and no launch-time assertion that
`NAV_HORIZON_STEPS ≤ episode_length`.

**The fix.**
1. **Make the route target non-circular** — v2.1 already does this (`route_from_future_v21`, curvature-
   relative, `ROUTE_UNKNOWN` masked not clamped). Ensure **no arm is ever again trained with
   `route_target(nav_cmd)`**; it is still the **default** in `refc_train.py --labels` (`08c48af` keeps
   `v1` as default for XL byte-reproducibility — correct for reproduction, dangerous as a default).
   Flip the default to `v21` and pin `v1` explicitly in the XL reproduction recipe.
2. **Add a label-coverage gate to the launch preflight**, next to the existing parity gate: assert
   label validity fraction on a 4,000-window sample exceeds a threshold, and assert every label horizon
   is ≤ the corpus clip length. `refc_train.py` already records label stats in `config.json`
   (`§4.3`: left 0.121 / straight 0.5645 / right 0.115 / UNKNOWN 0.1995) — **promote that from a
   record to a gate.**
3. **Add an anti-circularity test**: for every supervised head, assert the target is not a deterministic
   function of any fed input (mechanically checkable: mutual information between target and input-only
   features < 1.0 in the discrete case).
4. **Alarm on saturated metrics**: any accuracy at exactly 1.00 or any skill-vs-chance at exactly 0.0
   raises a loud warning in the trainer log.

- `CLAUDE.md` documents **two commit-sweep misattributions in one day** (`60265d3` swallowed the eval

3. **CI on push to `main`** plus a scheduled `origin/main` freshness alarm (it went 59 commits / 7 days
   stale silently).
4. **Deduplicate the trainers** (see W9) so a fix lands once.

### 🟠 W7 — The v3 rationale leans on LP-FT, but LP-FT's mechanism may not be available in this regime

**Evidence.** `TANITAD_PAPER.md §8` builds the two-stage v3 argument on Kumar et al. (ICLR 2022,
arXiv 2202.10054): full fine-tuning **distorts** pretrained features and underperforms a linear probe

**The problem.** Kumar's mechanism is *preservation of diverse, general pretrained features* that
fine-tuning would otherwise destroy. TanitAD's stage-1 candidate for `refa-v3` is
**own-driving-SSL-then-freeze on the same 2,376-episode single-source corpus** (`§9.3`). If stage 1 and
stage 2 use the *same narrow distribution*, there is **no feature diversity to preserve**, and the
theorem's OOD benefit does not transfer. The paper half-anticipates this ("unless stage-1
self-supervision is on our own driving corpus") but reads that condition as *enabling* the argument when
it is closer to *disabling* it: our own corpus is precisely the distribution we already fail to
generalize from (W3).

**Compounding:** the program's own data warns against the alternative. The pre-grounding JEPA latent had
a **1.65 m oracle decode ceiling** in-distribution vs 0.033 m for the grounded encoder (`§8`), so a
frozen trunk risks capping the metric far above end-to-end grounding. And the generic-frozen arm is
already run and plateaued (REF-A, 2.92 m).

**The fix.** Do not fund v3's frozen matrix on the LP-FT argument. Instead:
- **Make the de-risking ablation the decision.** §8 already names it: the α = 0.25 gradient-decouple is
  "the ≈1 %-cost ablation of this v3". Run it, gated on `fwd`-ADE non-regression + rising vision-use.
  **That is a ~1-day experiment that decides a multi-week direction.** Rank it P2 in §6.
- **If a frozen stage-1 is pursued, stage 1 must be trained on a corpus strictly broader than stage 2** —
  otherwise the LP-FT citation should be dropped from the rationale, because it is doing rhetorical work
  its assumptions do not support.

### 🟡 W10 — The "~92 % irreducible" argument is directionally right but overstated by one step

**What is established, and I accept it:** across 47 arms, the best recovery of the ranking gap **on the
training data itself** is 8.4 %; capacity is not binding (smaller heads are worse); dev tracks train
(`2026-07-20-refc-v12-learned-rescorer.md §4.4`). Combined with v1.0's 0.0 %, the GT-perfect
speed-matcher scoring *worse* than baseline, and the GT-perfect along-track ranker capping at 34 %, this
is strong evidence.

**Where it over-reaches.** The result bounds *what a re-scorer over a **fixed fan**, fed **that particular
feature set**, can recover*. It is not a bound on the irreducibility of the ADE, for two reasons the
registry's own conclusion half-acknowledges:
1. **The features may be starved, not the signal absent.** §4.3 finding 4 shows the frozen decoder
   embedding is "nearly worthless" (+0.6 points over geometry-only), and `q_source` barely separates.
   That is equally consistent with *"the frozen decoder's representation does not contain the
   discriminative information"* — a statement about the **trunk**, not about the world. The honest claim
   is: **the gap is not recoverable from the frozen decoder's own representation.**
2. **"Min over 256 vs one realised future" is a correct intuition but is never actually estimated.** The
   chance floors (13.96 m over the fan; 1.034 m in the top-8) bound the *lottery*, not the *aleatoric
   entropy of the future given the scene*.

**The estimator that would settle it (and I recommend it — it is cheap).** Estimate the aleatoric floor
**directly, model-free**, from repeated futures under near-identical contexts:
- Build context neighbourhoods: k-NN over the *observation* window (encoder latent or raw kinematics +
  scene descriptor), restricted to **different episodes** so the futures are genuinely independent draws.
- Within each neighbourhood, compute the **spread of realised futures** (mean pairwise ADE between
  realised trajectories, or the ADE of the neighbourhood's centroid trajectory to each member).
- That spread **is** the aleatoric floor: no observation-conditioned predictor can beat it, and the
  quantity `min-over-fan` should be compared to it, not to zero.
- Equivalently and more standard: report a **proper scoring rule** — minADE_k / CRPS / NLL under a
  calibrated fan — where aleatoric uncertainty is *modelled* rather than differenced away. This also
  makes the "is the fan a distribution or a menu?" question answerable.

**Verdict:** the operational conclusion — *stop funding selection research on REF-C* — stands and should
be kept. The *epistemic* claim ("92 % irreducible") should be restated as *"≤ 8.4 % is recoverable from
the frozen decoder's representation; the residual is dominated by future uncertainty, magnitude not yet
independently estimated."*

### 🟡 W11 — "Truncated 2-step diffusion" is iterative refinement; the label is a reviewer liability

**Evidence.** `stack/tanitad/refs/refc.py:570-576`: the refinement loop adds noise **only in training**
(`torch.zeros_like(x)` at eval), has **no variance/noise schedule**, no score-matching objective, and no

`:578-579` on `conf` from the **t = 0 classifier pass** (`:560`), because the refinement passes discard

publication, **yes**: calling it diffusion invites "where is your noise schedule, and what is the

**Related, and worth one line of code:** the registry's own recommendation is right — a trainer that
computes a confidence at every denoise pass and supervises **none of them** should either supervise all
passes or stop computing them (`refc-v12` §5.1 consequence 2).

### 🟡 W12 — Norm-parity monitoring (H26) is a heuristic that pointed at the wrong cause, and a lever justified by it is still live

**Evidence.** The H26 first verdict attributed the harmful intent→operative seam to *magnitude swamping*

## 5. The five most dangerous assumptions currently load-bearing

Ranked by *what breaks if the assumption is wrong*, not by likelihood.

### A1 — 🔴 "P2 validates the v3 architecture" — but P2 does not beat constant velocity

**The entire v3 pivot (D-033), the program's current direction, rests on P2.** P2's measured result is
**ADE@2s 0.893 ± 0.114** — and the registry's own leaderboard ranks it **8th, below the CV floor of
0.8248, marked "Beats CV ✗"** (`MODEL_REGISTRY.md §6`).

The comparison that justified the pivot is *relative*: planner 0.893 vs the supervised tactical head
3.150 (+2.257 m, CI-separated). That comparison is sound and "planning beats heads" is correct. But
**the head is a broken baseline** — it is 4× worse than constant velocity. Beating a broken baseline by
72 % establishes that the head is broken; it does **not** establish that the replacement is good. In
absolute terms **the architecture the program is pivoting toward currently loses to a zero-parameter
kinematic extrapolation**, while the architecture it is pivoting *away from* (v1's operative rollout,
0.452) beats every floor.

The program has the mechanistic explanation and it is probably right — P2's cost carries no
lateral/route term by design, 66 % of its residual is lateral, and true actions reach 0.452 on the same
windows (D-A10). **But that is a hypothesis about P3, not a result.**

**What breaks if this is wrong:** the whole v3 program. **Test that settles it:** P3 must take the
planner **below 0.825** open-loop. Until then the honest sentence — *"planning over the WM beats the
supervised head; it does not yet beat the kinematic floor"* — belongs in `PROGRAM_OVERVIEW §5.2(c)` and
paper §9, both of which currently present P2 as validating the direction without the floor comparison.

### A2 — 🔴 "Open-loop ADE is a usable steering metric"

The program *states* the correct position — open-loop numbers are "weak claims" (arXiv 2605.00066),
D1–D3 are "necessary-not-sufficient", "closed-loop D4–D6 arbitrate" (`TANITAD_PAPER.md §7.2`) — **and
then steers everything by open-loop ADE**: the leaderboard, the gates, v3enc's acceptance criteria, the
Phase-0 exit criterion (1), the "first sub-floor arm" headline. Its own measurement says the two
dissociate: 0.452 → **1.685** closed-loop, 22.2 % of windows diverging > 5 m.

**What breaks:** every ranking in registry §6 could reorder under closed-loop — including
flagship-vs-REF-C, separated by **0.006 m**, well inside any honest interval. **Note a structural
problem:** REF-B is *architecturally excluded* from the closed-loop harness (`§3`), so the H1 comparison
can never be arbitrated closed-loop as currently constructed. Worth solving before more REF-B compute.

### A3 — 🟠 "Results on the 2,376-episode single-source corpus transfer"

Already falsified in the specific, still the operating assumption in the general. One corpus, one camera
position, one provider — with a **known two-rig inconsistency inside it** (`§0.1`). OOD: loses to the
floor on both unseen corpora; path feasibility collapses to 62.8 %.

**What breaks:** C1 and C2 both. In particular **C2 — the data-efficiency slope, the Phase-1 headline —
cannot be measured on a single corpus at a single size.** It is the program's most important unbuilt
instrument. The two-tier dataset plan is the right response **only if the second tier enters TRAINING**;
today comma2k19 and Cosmos are staged for *eval only*.

### A4 — 🟠 "CI-separated is a reliable predicate"

Per W1 the single-arm intervals are ~20 % narrower than a correct cluster bootstrap, on 40 clusters. The
effects now being chased (+0.0089 m, +0.044) sit at or below what 40 episodes can resolve — the
refc-v12 note says so honestly and is right.

**What breaks:** marginal accept/reject calls flip. **Mitigation:** W1's fix, a larger val, and a
standing preference for **paired episode-clustered** tests, which the program already knows how to build
and which are largely immune to the defect.

### A5 — 🟠 "H4 is closed" — closed for one recipe, open for the recipe v3 adopts

`PROGRAM_OVERVIEW §3` records H4 as **"✅ CLOSED NEGATIVE"** unqualified. REF-A tested *frozen encoder +
**supervised regression heads***. The v3 direction is *frozen encoder + **feature-prediction of action
consequences + a planner**, no supervised head* — and the paper says so precisely: REF-A *"confounded the
frozen encoder with supervised-regression heads"* (`§9.3`).

Two program documents therefore disagree about whether the program's central open question is open. If
H4 reads closed, the v3 frozen matrix looks like relitigating a settled negative and gets
under-resourced; if open, REF-A's negative must stop being cited against it.

**Mitigation:** split it — **H4a (frozen + supervised heads) = REFUTED**, cleanly and publishably;
**H4b (frozen + feature-prediction + planning) = OPEN**, and it is the actual v3 question. Update
`PROGRAM_OVERVIEW §3` and the ledger to carry both rows.

## 6. Chief-scientist proposals, ranked by expected impact per GPU-day

The top items cost **zero GPU-days** and several *save* GPU-weeks, so their impact-per-GPU-day is
unbounded. That is the ranking, not a rhetorical flourish.

### P1 — Replace the restart gate. **0 GPU-days; saves an estimated 3–8 GPU-days immediately.** 🔴
Implement W2: held-out milestone metric, matched-step ratio, episode-cluster bootstrap CI, one
pre-registered gate step with a numeric threshold, budget-normalized to GPU-hours, two-restarts-per-
lever-family cap.
**Falsifiable success criterion:** the next restart/continue decision is made on a statistic whose CI is
reported, whose reference arms were measured over identical step ranges, and which was written down
*before* launch. **Immediate application: do not kill v3enc on its pre-5k exponent** — run to the 10 k
milestone and judge on the three pre-registered val gates that already exist
(`2026-07-19-flagshipv2-6k-diagnostic.md:196-199`).

### P2 — Fix the interval estimator and rename the statistic. **0 GPU-days.** 🔴
W1: episode-cluster bootstrap (B = 2000) for single-arm CIs, honest naming, estimator unit tests, and
the merged D1 power audit (`35d1041`) put to work.
**Falsifiable success criterion:** on a synthetic benchmark with known ground truth the new interval
achieves 93–97 % empirical coverage and the old one is shown to under-cover. Then re-issue registry §6
and state which conclusions change. *Prediction: flagship-vs-REF-C (0.4522 vs 0.458) ceases to be
separable — which is the honest result.*

### P3 — Run the α = 0.25 gradient-decouple ablation; let it decide v3's stage-1 question.
**~1 GPU-day.** 🔴
The paper already names it *"the ≈1 %-cost ablation of this v3"* (`§8`). Gate exactly as §8 specifies.
**Falsifiable success criterion:** vision_use rises **> 20 %** (from ~12 %) while `fwd`-ADE holds within
10 % of 0.033 m → fund the two-phase freeze. If the metric collapses when the encoder is unshackled → a
frozen v3 hits the 1.65 m ceiling and **stage 1 must be solved before any v3 training is funded.**
Either outcome saves weeks. *Best science-per-GPU-day item on the board.*

### P4 — Estimate the aleatoric floor directly, model-free. **0 GPU-days (CPU + cached features).** 🟠
W10's estimator: k-NN context neighbourhoods restricted to *different episodes*; measure the spread of
realised futures; report minADE_k / CRPS alongside ADE.
**Falsifiable success criterion:** produce `σ_aleatoric@2s` with a bootstrap CI. Every arm then gets a
principled denominator, "92 % irreducible" becomes quantitative, and the oracle-in-fan gap becomes
interpretable. **Secondary payoff:** it reveals how much of the 0.452 → 0.500 margin is even improvable
— which nobody currently knows.

### P5 — Promote the OOD panel to the binding acceptance gate. **0 GPU-days.** 🟠
Enforce D-A8. No arm is accepted, and no registry row reads "✅", without an OOD row.
**Falsifiable success criterion:** the next accepted arm reaches comma2k19 floor win-rate ≥ 35 % (from
17.5 %). If no arm can clear it, that is itself the finding and it redirects the program to data (P7)
rather than to more levers.

### P6 — Close the label-defect class permanently. **0 GPU-days.** 🟠
W4: flip `refc_train.py --labels` default to `v21`; add a launch-time **label-coverage gate** (promote
`config.json` label stats from record to gate); add an **anti-circularity test**; alarm on any accuracy
at exactly 1.00 or skill-vs-chance at exactly 0.0; assert every label horizon ≤ corpus clip length.
**Falsifiable success criterion:** re-running the preflight against the *historical* v1 configuration
must **fail** both the coverage gate and the circularity test. If it passes, the gates are too weak.

### P7 — Get a second corpus into **training**, not just evaluation. **High cost — and it is the binding
constraint.** 🟠
Every generalization claim, C2's slope, and the OOD gate bottleneck here. comma2k19 (MIT) and
Cosmos-Drive-Dreams (CC-BY-4.0) are already staged, canonicalized and licence-cleared.
**Falsifiable success criterion:** a two-corpus-trained arm at matched params/steps must (a) hold
in-distribution ADE within 10 % of 0.452 and (b) lift comma2k19 floor win-rate above 35 %. **If (b)
fails even with comma in training, the deficit is architectural, not data** — a major, cheap finding.
**Caveat:** this breaks `e438721ae894`, so it must be a *new* parity key and a *new* arm family, never a
silent re-selection (CLAUDE.md: "Parity is sacred").

### P8 — P3-planner: put the lateral/route term in the cost. **~2 GPU-days (mostly eval).** 🟠
D-A10 localizes it: 66 % of P2's residual is lateral; curved-window error 2.114 m vs 0.484 m with true
actions.
**Falsifiable success criterion (this is A1's test):** the planner must go **below the CV floor of
0.825** open-loop and hold or improve G4 closed-loop (1.038). If the lateral term does not clear the
floor, the v3 thesis needs re-examination *before* v3 training is funded — which is exactly when you
want to know.

### P9 — Structural fixes to the working system. **0 GPU-days; prevents the largest historical loss
class.** 🟠
W5 + W9: per-agent branches (kill the shared index), pod fingerprint + pod-side pytest recorded in every
`config.json`, `pod_git_drift.py` **blocking at launch**, an `origin/main` freshness alarm, and
deduplication of the nine trainers into `tanitad/` libraries with thin arm-specific mains.
**Falsifiable success criterion:** a scheduled audit finds **zero** artifacts existing in only one place,
and a deliberately-drifted pod is *refused* at launch rather than discovered in an audit.

### P10 — Cheap evaluation hardening. **0 GPU-days.** 🟡
Enlarge canonical val toward 80–100 episodes; make the train/val leak assertion **fatal** in `bench.py`;
record drive/geography/rig composition of the split; retarget `gate-eval` off the dead
`p0-sB01-realmix`; rewrite `LEADERBOARD.md` in metric-BEV units from registry §6.
**Falsifiable success criterion:** an intentionally leaked arm is refused by the harness, and the val's
rig balance is a published number.

### P11 — Publication hygiene. **0 GPU-days.** 🟡
Restate the abstract's claim ladder with the bounded claim first (W3); rename "diffusion" to "anchored
iterative refinement (DiffusionDrive-style truncated diffusion)" (W11); retire "deployed"; split H4 into
H4a/H4b (A5); re-gate or mark superseded the stale D2/D3 rows; restate "92 % irreducible" in its bounded
form (W10).
**Falsifiable success criterion:** a reader of the abstract alone cannot come away believing a
driving-competence or corpus-generality claim has been made.
