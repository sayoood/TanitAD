## Executive summary (honest — P8)

- **The week's pivot is the speed/scale reset (2026-07-14).** Root cause found: *none* of the three
  arms received the **current ego-speed `v0`** as an input, so each was trying to infer absolute speed
  from vision — which a frozen encoder provably cannot (speed-probe R² ceilinged at 0.61). This capped
  **every** arm, not just REF-A. Fix = append `v0` as a 3rd action channel. In isolation it **halved
  REF-A's driving error (3.73 → 2.14 m held-out)** and lifted speed-decodability **0.61 → 0.965 R²**.
  All three arms **restarted from scratch** with speed + jerk + aux-accel. This supersedes the entire
  pre-reset narrative in the W31 `PROJECT_STATE` (the "14.2-vs-7.5 confounded head-to-head").
- **The driving-capability gap now has an honest denominator.** Benchmarks shipped a tested best-of-3
  kinematic floor (CV / go-straight / CTRV, speed-gated strata) measured on **26,132 real anchors**:
  the honest floor is **~0.056 m @1s** (CTRV wins 55–58 %), *not* the single-CV 0.28 m the diagnostic
  had used. Against it, the frozen flagship anchor (6.44 m) is **~115× the floor** — D1-FAIL is
  reinforced, and neither reset arm (REF-A 2.14 m / flagship 2.34 m@5k) yet beats even the trivial
  baseline open-loop. **This is the top program risk, unchanged and now sharper.**
- **Encoder signal is real, verdict still pending.** At the 1 s horizon the trained-encoder flagship at

  sharper near-term at ⅐ the training. The fair verdict is **flagship @30k** (~Jul 19–23).
