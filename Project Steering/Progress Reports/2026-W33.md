### 2.2 The two results that matter scientifically

**(a) Genuine prediction, proven causally (in-distribution).** TanitEval generalization panel, 881 windows:
on high-CTRV-divergence windows (upcoming turns/brakes CTRV cannot extrapolate) the model beats the CTRV
**oracle** by **+0.796 m on 94 %** of them — and mean-replacing the scene **inverts** it to −0.529 m.
**Vision-effect +1.325 m, CI [+1.04, +1.64]** (separated). Support: advantage is monotone-increasing with
divergence (Q1 −0.372 → Q4 +0.796); upcoming road curvature linearly decodable from the pooled latent
**R² 0.254 vs 0.031 ego-only (+0.223)**; predicted paths physically shaped 95.9 % vs GT 97.1 %; occluding
road-ahead shifts the prediction **1.60×** more than periphery. **Honest limits:** it dynamics-guesses on
near-inertial windows (correct — CTRV *is* optimal there) and **loses to CTRV on the top-10 % high-speed
(−0.617 m)**.

**(b) OOD says it is partly distribution-fit.** Same ckpt on newly-staged unseen corpora:

| Corpus | n | model ADE@2s | floor | beats floor % | read |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---|

| comma2k19 (OOD real hwy) | 2 176 | 0.849 | 0.372 | 17.5 % | ✗ |
| cosmos (OOD synthetic) | 92 | 0.583 | 0.358 | 29.4 % | ✗ |

Both OOD sets are highway-dominated → a very strong CTRV floor (~0.37 m). Model error roughly **doubles**
OOD and the anticipation advantage collapses **0.80 → 0.15 m**; path feasibility drops **97.8 % → 62.8 %**
on OOD sharp curvature. **But vision-ablation still hurts on comma (0.27 m, CI-sep)** — it reads the OOD
scene, it just cannot net-beat that floor. This is now the pre-registered baseline v2/v3 must lift.
