## P1 — Cosine pre-probe: surgery REFUTED → recommend FROM-SCRATCH (MEASURED, the decider)

**What was measured.** At the single `states` seam that couples the planner into the shared trunk
(`train_flagship_v4.v4_loss_step`), on **v4.2b's ckpt (step 4000)** over **512 windows** of the CLEAN val
split `physicalai-val-0c5f7dac3b11` (0 skipped), the per-window geometry of
`g_wm = ∂L_predict/∂states` vs `g_plan = ∂L_plan/∂states` — using the staged one-sided-PCGrad
`deconflict()` (`grad_surgery.py`). `g_plan` at `lambda_plan=1.0` (raw); cosine & frac_removed are
scale-invariant, fp32 (training ran bf16 — the directional verdict is precision-robust).
Instrument + raw output: `cosine_preprobe.py`, `preprobe_v42b.json` (this dir).

| quantity (over 512 windows) | value | reading |
|---|---|---|
| **seam cosine mean** | **+0.0043** (sd 0.0640) | g_wm ⟂ g_plan — essentially **zero** directional conflict |
| cosine p05 / min | −0.0971 / −0.2731 | even the worst windows barely oppose |
| cosine p50 / p95 | +0.0027 / +0.1078 | distribution centered at 0, symmetric |
| frac windows cos<0 | 0.479 | ~half±half → orthogonality **noise**, not a real conflict |
| **seam frac_removed mean** | **0.0224** (median **0.0**, p95 0.097) | surgery would strip **~2 %** of ‖g_plan‖ |
| ‖g_wm‖ / ‖g_plan‖ mean | 0.125 / 0.030 | raw planner grad is **~4.2× weaker** than the WM grad |

**Interpretation — this is PRE_REGISTRATION World #3 (conflict NOT directional).**
One-sided PCGrad only ever subtracts the *conflicting projection* of `g_plan`. With a near-zero cosine
that projection is negligible (**2.2 %**), so surgery is ≈ a **no-op** vs today's summed backward — it
**cannot** rescue the WM canary that the scalar floor failed to hold. Two independent angles agree:

1. **Geometry:** cos ≈ 0, frac_removed 2.2 % → there is almost no conflicting component to remove. Not
   the `cos ≈ −1` fundamental-opposition case, and not a `mildly-negative with a *substantive*
   removable conflict` case — the removable conflict is negligible.
2. **Magnitude sanity:** floor-0.15 already attenuated the **entire** planner gradient to 15 % and the
   canary still degraded to 0.70. Surgery passes **~98 %** of `g_plan` (drops only the 2 % conflicting
   part) — i.e. **more** planner coupling than the floor, so it cannot possibly hold the WM better than
   the floor did. Whatever degrades the canary is **not** the planner gradient's magnitude *or*
   direction.

⇒ **Gradient surgery is REFUTED as the lever for the v4.2b degradation** (the "kill the experiment for
free" outcome PRE_REGISTRATION §2/§4 Phase-0 was built to detect). Do **not** spend the ~1.3 A40-day
surgery experiment.

**Recommendation (which arm): FROM-SCRATCH.** `--from-scratch` (the READY v4 fallback, `a05a5c9e`):
WM + anchored-diffusion planner **co-evolve from random init** = the v1 training regime that held the
canary at 0.42 (v1 is the existence proof). It sidesteps the warm-start coupling entirely. Brief cost
