# TanitAD Program Improvements from the External Survey

## (1) Per-arm concrete improvements

### Flagship / flagship-v2 (261M 4-brain) — survey techniques vs our 6 queued levers

| flagship-v2 lever | Survey verdict | Detail |
|---|---|---|
| **v0+yr0 → planning brains** | **VALIDATED** by PLAN-S | PLAN-S's dual AdaFiLM conditions its cost maps on the ego's *current kinematic state* — exactly our lever, published with a 42% relative 3s-collision reduction on nuScenes. ColaVLA's "ego-adaptive token selection" is a second independent confirmation that ego-state must reach the planning stage. Keep the lever as-is; FiLM-style injection (not concat) is the externally-converged form (also matches our 2026-07-08 AdaLN>FiLM triangulation). |
| **Future-action dropout** | **VALIDATED in goal, different mechanism** (ColaVLA) | ColaVLA attacks the same disease (exposure bias / teacher-forcing leakage) by eliminating autoregression entirely — parallel single-pass decode. Since our step-readout decode is already parallel, our dropout lever is the training-side complement, not a substitute. No change needed. |
| **rollout-k 12** | **VALIDATED by WorldRFT, WARNED by DreamerAD + our own data** | WorldRFT needs long latent rollouts as its RFT environment — k=12 is the right substrate for the post-CARLA fine-tune (below). But DreamerAD's Shortcut Forcing exists precisely because recursive multi-step imagination degrades — and we *measured* that degradation ourselves (σ dissipation, fidelity→chance by k4, 2026-07-17 ledger entry). Recommendation: keep k=12 for the predictor loss, but train it shortcut-style (direct multi-resolution jumps: 1, 2, 4, 8, 12 as separate targets / self-distilled big steps) rather than pure recursion. This converts our "cap at 1-step/parallel-horizon" defensive constraint into an offensive training recipe. |
| **Goal-conditioned decode** | **VALIDATED exactly by ColaVLA; EXTENDED by LAMP and PLAN-S** | ColaVLA is our finding published: a compact decision-level latent ("meta-action embedding") used as a *global prior* for a single-forward-pass hierarchical planner, with SOTA open-loop + 53% closed-loop NeuroNCAP gain and >5× latency cut. This is strong external confirmation that goal-latent cos 0.885 + weak decode means *invest in the decoder, the goal representation is fine*. Extensions: LAMP → make the goal partially discrete (codebook index, next row); PLAN-S → decode through an interpretable cost-map intermediary instead of straight latent→coords. |
| **Nav dropout** | **EXTENDED by LAMP** | LAMP uses lane topology not as an input but as a *feasibility filter* over candidate intentions (rejects off-road/unreachable modes before decode). That is the robust complement to nav dropout: even when nav is dropped, a topology filter keeps decoded trajectories structurally feasible. Cheap version for us: route-ctx-derived reachability mask over tactical waypoint proposals. |
| **TMS jerk penalty** | **VALIDATED in goal, EXTENDED architecturally by BridgeAD** | BridgeAD (CVPR 2025, real) makes temporal plan coherence *architectural*: historical predictions for the current frame feed perception, historical plans feed current planning. Our jerk penalty is the loss-side version; BridgeAD suggests the input-side version — persist the previous step's rollout/plan latent and condition the current plan on it (with dropout so it can't become a crutch — composes naturally with our dropout levers). Directly measurable with our existing trajectory-seam and TMS instruments. |

Additional flagship notes: our imagination-real-but-modest (8.7%) and vision_use 12.9% numbers get context from ColaVLA/CLOVER's insistence that open-loop L2 cannot arbitrate such contributions (consistent with our 2605.00066 ledger entry) — the closed-loop CARLA W31-32 milestone is where these edges either cash out or die; don't spend flagship compute chasing open-loop ADE below CTRV 0.544 when the ego-status-shortcut floor is 0.66.

### REF-A (frozen-encoder arms, ceiling 2.14m, vision inert)
- **VERDI-pattern adapter distillation** is the cheapest credible attack on the frozen ceiling: keep DINO frozen, train *progressive feature projectors* aligned (cosine) to a stronger teacher's intermediate features — lifts representation without unfreezing. This is a Phase-1 H4 refinement, not a Phase-0 spend.
- **LAMP primitives as a diagnostic**: classification over a discrete trajectory codebook demands less metric precision from features than free regression. If a codebook-selector head lifts REF-A tactical more than flagship tactical, the representation-vs-decode bottleneck is cleanly localized per arm. Cheap probe-style experiment (frozen ckpt, head-only).

### REF-B (planner arm, refbpatch: fixed-distance path head + ego-dropout + aux-yaw)
- **PerlAD directly validates REF-B's decomposition**: its hierarchical decoupled planner is IL for the *lateral path* + RL for the *longitudinal speed* — our fixed-distance path head is the same lateral/longitudinal split. When RL arrives, REF-B is the natural first RL host (longitudinal-only RL on a frozen path head is the low-risk entry).
- **CLOVER scorer head**: grow REF-B from single-plan to candidate-set + lightweight scorer trained on planning sub-scores (collision/progress/comfort — we already have TMS/LAL/violation-rate as sub-scores). Fixes the imitation-mismatch CLOVER documents: safe deviations from the logged path stop being penalized.
- **LAMP topology filter** applies here first (path head is where infeasible modes appear).

### REF-C (proven-architecture arm, being built)
- The survey answers "which proven architecture": **BridgeAD** is the strongest candidate — peer-reviewed (CVPR 2025), a complete published recipe, and its core idea (historical prediction/plan bridging) is orthogonal enough to our hierarchy to be a real control rather than a sibling. Alternative: a minimal WorldRFT-style latent planner (AAAI, code lineage CAS/Li Auto). Avoid basing REF-C on any of the unverified-2026 papers (section 3).

### Closed-loop / CARLA plan (W31-32)
- **Think2Drive is the cost anchor**: expert-level on CARLA-v2's 39 corner cases in ~3 days on a single GPU via latent-space RL. Our operative predictor is architecturally the same substrate — budget the CARLA RFT phase at pod-days, not pod-weeks.
- **WorldRFT GRPO is the concrete post-BC algorithm**: trajectory Gaussianization + collision-aware rewards inside latent rollouts, group-relative (no critic — cheap). Slot it as the first post-BC fine-tune once CARLA lands.
- **PerlAD's pseudo-simulation** is the bridge *before* full CARLA: a prediction-WM that makes surrounding agents reactive to the ego plan, entirely in vector space — we could assemble a v0 from our own operative predictor + the Opponent Analyzer scenario suite (SC-01/SC-06/SC-13 already exist as scripted contrasts) without any rendering.

### Edge (Orin-class, fp16 93.7 Hz, predictor launch-bound)
- **LayerNorm→bounded-tanh + ERF→tanh** matter only for DLA offload; since our measured bottleneck is *kernel-launch overhead*, CUDA-graph capture / kernel fusion outranks the tanh-LN swap for now. File tanh-LN as the known recipe for when we target DLA residency; don't retrofit pre-architecture-freeze.
- **KD-before-INT8 is the one edge claim to adopt as doctrine now**: direct PTQ of the final model is documented to collapse; a distilled student preserves quantization robustness. Consequence: budget a small distillation pass into the Phase-1 edge plan, and keep REF-C/flagship checkpoints as future teachers.
- **Fast-dDrive scaffold speculative decoding** is only relevant if the H12 1B-bridge ships with autoregressive text output — in that case it's the latency fix. Our trajectory decode is already parallel (ColaVLA-consistent); nothing to do there.

### Data Engineering (feeds OWN_DATASET_PLAN)
- **ACID-style active curation, oracle-gap variant**: ACID selects batches maximizing the student-vs-reference gap. We have a *free* reference: the CTRV/best-of-3 kinematic floor and per-stratum skill_score already built by Benchmarks & Eval. Weight sampling by the model-vs-oracle deficit per stratum — this directly attacks comma being 73.9% straight (Benchmarks' own finding). Caveat: ACID (CVPR 2025) is generic multimodal-distillation work, not AD-specific — cite it as the principle, not as driving evidence.
- **AIDE-pattern VLM auto-labeling**: the WorldModel-Synthetic corpus's Qwen captions (already flagged usable-now, 2026-07-15) are exactly the semantic index AIDE builds — a long-tail scenario *retrieval* layer over our corpora is a weekend script, not a research program.

## (2) NEW hypothesis candidates (H19+)

- **H19 — Discrete tactical vocabulary.** A VQ-VAE codebook of trajectory primitives (LAMP-style) as the tactical brain's output space beats continuous waypoint regression under speed-starved training. *Falsifier:* matched-compute head swap fails to cut tactical wp ADE ≥20% from 3.38m, or codebook selection accuracy fails to transfer to decoded metric ADE. *Gate:* held-out tactical wp ADE < 2.7m with no operative-brain regression; codebook utilization > 50% (no collapse).
- **H20 — Plan-persistence bridging.** Conditioning the current plan on the previous frame's predictions (BridgeAD) improves temporal coherence *and* accuracy, beyond what the TMS jerk penalty buys. *Falsifier:* no improvement on trajectory-seam/TMS at equal ADE, or reactivity degrades on the Stationary-Lead scenario (SC-13 collision contrast must stay 0.0). *Gate:* seam metric improvement + ade@2s non-regress + SC-13 pass.
- **H21 — Latent reinforcement fine-tuning.** GRPO with collision-aware rewards inside our own operative rollouts (WorldRFT) improves closed-loop safety over the BC checkpoint. *Falsifier:* collision rate drops in latent rollouts but not in CARLA (reward-hacking the world model's blind spots — our σ-dissipation finding says imagined states go overconfident, so this failure mode is live), or no CI-separated DS gain over ≥3 seeds at matched wall-clock. *Gate:* CARLA DS/collision, mean±CI over ≥3 seeds, vs BC ckpt.
- **H22 — Shortcut-trained imagination.** Multi-resolution step-compression training (DreamerAD Shortcut Forcing) fixes the measured k-step σ dissipation that currently caps H15's self-monitor at 1-step. *Falsifier:* shortcut-trained ImaginationField still collapses to the attractor (hidden-cell fidelity → chance by k4) or σ still shrinks while error grows. *Gate:* D8/D9 — hidden-cell fidelity ≥ 0.25 at k4 and positive σ↔error rank correlation.
- **H23 — Interpretable cost-map decode head.** A PLAN-S-style multi-channel cost-map aux head on the tactical latent adds risk observability (H9/H11 synergy) without costing accuracy. *Falsifier:* cost-map channels fail to predict actual CARLA collisions/off-road events (low AUROC), or D1 regresses. *Gate:* violation-rate AUROC > 0.8 + D1 non-regress. (Needs BEV pseudo-labels — Cosmos-DD/PandaSet tier, not comma.)
- **H24 — Oracle-gap curriculum.** Sampling weighted by per-stratum model-vs-kinematic-oracle deficit (ACID/Den-TP principle + our CTRV floor) beats uniform sampling at matched steps. *Falsifier:* no per-stratum skill_score lift, or straight-line/highway performance regresses (distribution-shift tax exceeds curvature gain). *Gate:* per-stratum skill_score improvement at matched compute with aggregate non-regress.

## (4) Ranked top-5 "do next"

1. **Oracle-gap curriculum (H24)** — data-only, zero architecture risk, attacks the known 73.9%-straight skew with instruments we already own (CTRV floor + strata). Cost: **4060, ~half a day** for sampler stats + A/B config; then a **free rider on the flagship-v2 pod retrain** (run the A/B on eval-pod at 5k steps first to protect arm parity).
2. **Tactical VQ codebook (H19)** — attacks our single worst number (tactical 3.38m) with the survey's best-matched technique; trajectory data is tiny so the VQ-VAE is cheap. Cost: **4060, 1–2 days** codebook + head; **one eval-pod ablation (~5k steps)** for the head-swap; run the REF-A-vs-flagship diagnostic variant as a frozen-ckpt probe (**Colab/4060, hours**).
3. **Plan-persistence bridging (H20)** — smallest code delta with a published architecture behind it (BridgeAD); composes with the already-queued dropout levers; measurable immediately with TMS/seam + SC-13. Cost: **4060 smoke ~1 day**, then lever #7 in flagship-v2 or the first post-v2 ablation (**pod, shared run**).
4. **Shortcut-forcing imagination retrain (H22)** — fixes a *measured* failure (σ dissipation) that currently blocks the H11/D8 multi-step self-monitor; DreamerAD provides the recipe. Cost: **4060-scale first** on the 22M-param ImaginationField (~1–2 days), pod integration only after the falsifier passes at small scale.
5. **GRPO-RFT prep (H21)** — highest ceiling, gated on CARLA W31-32; do the prep now so the fine-tune starts the day CARLA lands: reward stack (Gaussianized trajectories + collision-aware terms) wired into the existing latent-rollout harness. Cost: **eval-pod, ~2 days prep**; the fine-tune itself budgeted at **pod-days** (Think2Drive anchor: 3 days, 1 GPU). Guard: H22 should land first or the RFT will optimize against overconfident imagined states.

Near-misses: PLAN-S cost-map head (H23) — high interpretability value but blocked on BEV pseudo-labels (Cosmos-DD/PandaSet tier, and PandaSet is D-016-geometry-blocked); VERDI adapter distillation for REF-A — right idea, Phase-1 window.
